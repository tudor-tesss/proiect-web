Bucharest is the capital and largest city of Romania, as well as its cultural, industrial, and financial centre. It is located in the southeast of the country, at 44°25′57″N 26°06′14″E, on the banks of the Dâmbovița River, less than 60 km north of the Danube River and the Bulgarian border. Its elevation is of 84 m (276 ft) above sea level. According to the 2011 census, the city proper has a population of 1,883,425, within a metropolitan area of 2,183,091 inhabitants, while the urban area has a population of 1,883,425. The city is located in the southeast of Romania, at the crossroads between the historical regions of Dacia and Wallachia. Its territory is organized in 6 sectors. The historical centre of Bucharest was first mentioned in documents in 1459. It was first mentioned under its current name in 1534. The name comes from the old German name Bucza or Buczacz, which in turn comes from the Slavic name Buka.
